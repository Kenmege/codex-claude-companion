# Codex Plugin CC X Launch Video Design

**Status:** Approved by Kennedy on 2026-07-11

## Goal

Create a 55–60 second, proof-first launch film for Codex Plugin CC v1.1.1 that makes one product truth instantly clear: the originating Codex task remains the control plane while Claude performs a full coding job in a separate terminal, after which Codex verifies the result.

The film must feel like a premium founder-led product reveal, but its credibility comes from real terminal evidence rather than a simulated interface. Generated media frames and amplifies the demonstration; it never replaces the product proof.

## Audience and desired reaction

The primary audience is developers already using Codex, Claude Code, or both. The first-view reaction should be:

1. “That is not another review wrapper.”
2. “Codex is still active while Claude codes in another terminal.”
3. “I can inspect and control the handoff.”
4. “The final result is verified, not merely generated.”

The title is **I Gave Codex a Second Coding Brain**. The launch line is **ONE PROMPT. TWO ELITE CODING MINDS.**

## Approved treatment

Use a founder-led proof film with a short photoreal Kennedy opening, a real split-terminal coding demonstration as the body, and a Kennedy close over the verified result. This treatment was selected over a terminal-only “duel” and a founder manifesto because it combines human authority with product evidence.

The public cut must not promise virality, “every model,” autonomous perfection, or results not shown in the footage. Model names may appear only when captured from the live session used for the demonstration. GPT-side model copy remains **active Codex model** because the plugin inherits the originating task rather than pinning a model identifier.

## Proof boundary

The following elements must be captured from a real, sanitized run:

- the originating Codex task and its coding request;
- the separate Claude control-panel terminal;
- file changes made by the Claude worker;
- the controlled failing test and subsequent passing test;
- Codex inspecting the diff and running verification;
- the final session and test receipts.

The demonstration uses a disposable public fixture with invented names and no credentials, private repository paths, tokens, account details, or unrelated terminal history. Terminal text is never recreated as generated video. If the live run differs from the script, narration and callouts must be revised to match the captured evidence.

## Visual identity

The visual register is **human authority meeting technical precision**.

- Background: warm graphite (`#0C0E12`), never pure black.
- Foreground: warm paper (`#F3F0E8`), adjusted lighter weights for dark-background legibility.
- Primary accent: ion blue (`#6078FF`) used only for the Codex-to-Claude handoff, active focus, and structural rules.
- Status green: inherited from real test output only; it is not a decorative brand color.
- Founder imagery: natural skin texture, practical warm key light, restrained cool rim light, shallow but believable depth of field.
- Display type: **Bricolage Grotesque** at 800–900 weight for founder statements and launch lines.
- Technical type: **IBM Plex Mono** at 400–600 weight for session labels, commands, receipts, and data.

Type is anchored to frame edges and sized for video, not laid out like a web page. Headlines occupy 60–80% of the usable width. On-screen copy stays shorter than its available reading time. No gradient text, generic glass cards, cyberpunk grids, fake holograms, or decorative model logos.

## Motion language

Motion communicates the workflow hierarchy:

- Kennedy’s entrance is restrained and cinematic: a slow focus resolve with one subtle camera push.
- The first terminal reveal is a confident directional push, matching the physical move into the proof.
- The Codex-to-Claude handoff uses a single ion-blue structural line that crosses the frame and resolves into the second terminal.
- Related proof beats use short push transitions; the verification climax uses the fastest zoom-through accent; the founder close returns through a gentle focus pull.
- Each scene has a build, breathe, and resolve phase. Entrance timing varies by weight and importance. Non-final scenes remain fully visible until their transition handles the exit.
- Captions use conversational groups of three to five words, one group at a time, with deterministic frame timing and a hard end for every group.

Motion remains deterministic and seekable. Generated camera movement is reserved for the founder/background plates; UI motion is derived only from the real recording and frame-based overlays.

## Production source allocation

- **HeyGen:** opening and closing Kennedy performance using the approved private casual T-shirt reference. The private source image and provider identifiers are not committed.
- **ElevenLabs:** founder voice only if the approved avatar recording does not provide a sufficiently natural take. The final mix uses one consistent voice.
- **Real screen capture:** every product interaction, terminal command, code change, test receipt, and session status.
- **GPT Image:** photoreal founder key art and environmental plates derived from the approved private reference.
- **Runway:** subtle camera motion or background transition plates only; never terminal reconstruction or lip-sync proof.
- **HyperFrames:** title design, handoff line, proof callouts, and deterministic transition studies.
- **Remotion:** authoritative edit, audio sync, captions, safe-area adaptation, and multi-format renders.

## Deliverables

1. `1080 × 1350` 4:5 X feed master, 30 fps, 55–60 seconds.
2. `1920 × 1080` 16:9 landscape proof cut.
3. `1080 × 1920` 9:16 teaser derived from the same approved timeline.
4. Burned-in accessible captions plus a matching `.srt` sidecar.
5. A clean poster frame with Kennedy, the split-terminal proof, and the launch line.
6. Final X post and optional six-post technical thread, held for a separate public-post approval.

## Acceptance gates

- Kennedy’s identity, skin texture, hands, clothing, and eye line remain natural and consistent.
- No face crop, mouth artifact, background leak, unreadable terminal text, fake UI, or invented receipt survives review.
- The originating Codex task and the separate Claude terminal are visibly distinct at the decisive handoff.
- The live demonstration proves a writable coding job, a test cycle, Codex verification, and a green result.
- Every product claim matches the public v1.1.1 release and the captured session.
- Captions do not cover Kennedy’s face or the command/output being proven.
- The film is understandable muted, and the spoken mix remains clear on laptop and phone speakers.
- A frame-by-frame secret and privacy review finds no credential, user path, account identifier, provider balance, or private repository name.
- Public posting remains paused until Kennedy approves the final render and post copy.
